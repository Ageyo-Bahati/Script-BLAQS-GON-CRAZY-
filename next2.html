Scene – “Nyambura’s Confession” (Sh*t Gets Real)

Jaymo: (lighting a cigarette) "You always pull me back in, Nyambura. Like I’m some kind of idiot."

Nyambura: (softly) "Maybe... you just can’t help but love me."

Jaymo: "Loving you feels like holding fire in my hands. And I ain’t even mad about the burn."

(Nyambura shifts closer, her hand brushing against his thigh. There's something different about her tonight—less sharp, more vulnerable.)

Nyambura: (quietly) "I never planned any of this, Jaymo. Scamming you, playing you... it was supposed to be just a game."

(Jaymo turns, looking her dead in the eyes.)

Jaymo: "Then what the fuck changed?"

Nyambura: (whispering) "You. You changed the whole fucking script."

(Jaymo feels his heart drop, and for once, the smooth-talking lawyer doesn’t know what to say. He tosses his cigarette and pulls her into a rough, desperate kiss. It's not pretty—it’s messy, raw, and real, like two people drowning and grabbing onto each other for air.)

Jaymo: (murmuring against her lips) "We’re gonna burn everything down, aren’t we?"

Nyambura: (grinning) "Let’s burn, baby. Burn it all."

Nyambura: "I know I’m poison, Jaymo. But you’re the only one who knows how to drink me without dying."

Jaymo: (softly) "You’re gonna be the end of me, girl."

Nyambura: (whispering) "Then let’s end together."

Jaymo: (yelling) "Duck, niggas!"

Nyambura: (grinning) "I told you—drama follows me everywhere."
